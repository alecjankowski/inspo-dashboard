Culture Radar
What's the signal?

The origin story of play is being rewritten — 12,000-year-old Native American dice push back gambling's origin by 6,000 years, while Czech animation's Metrograph retrospective frames puppetry and stop-motion as foundational play objects — both pointing toward a cultural appetite for pre-digital craft and chance.

Color as psychological infrastructure is having a moment — the seafoam green control room deep dive and the moody, limited palette of Cinga Samson's paintings both make the case that color is never decorative — it's always doing emotional labor we don't consciously register.

Character design is re-emerging as a serious cultural signal — Pictoplasma Berlin 2026 arrives right as character-led brand work and mascot culture are accelerating, suggesting the pendulum is swinging back from pure typography/minimalism toward expressive, creature-forward identity systems.
