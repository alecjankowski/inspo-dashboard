Culture Radar
Patterns from the full scan — not just what made the cut.

Slow-built, hand-made work is beating the algorithm — a grandmother's 1940s sketches going viral, Brad Bird's 30-year passion project finally arriving, and Kamiya's analogue-digital illustration practice all point to the same thing: audiences are craving work that couldn't have been made quickly.

Independent creators are dethroning the gatekeepers — YouTube's new data on indie animators shows Hazbin Hotel and Amazing Digital Circus reshaping the industry from outside it, while FSG's shutdown of MCD Books (via Kottke) marks another institutional collapse — distributed creative power is winning.

Brands are becoming cultural infrastructure — Auckland Transport's hip-hop campaign positions a bus route as neighborhood identity, echoing how Vacation × Soft Services packaging builds desire through sensory storytelling — the best brand work today doesn't advertise, it participates.
